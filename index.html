Language Models, Information, Networks, Knowledge, and Society

Working across disciplines to understand how language models are reshaping the information environment, public discourse, and society.

About

The Workshop on Language Models, Information, Networks, Knowledge, and Society (LINKS) brings together researchers from NLP, machine learning, and the social sciences to examine how large language models are reshaping the information environments that underpin society. Using the information environment as an integrating frame, the workshop spans information-seeking and epistemic autonomy; political bias and civic discourse; social media, misinformation, and AI agents; and the audits, evaluation, and governance needed to study and steward these systems responsibly.
